WHAT WE DO

Preschoolers begin to develop basic thinking concepts, including notions of quantity, similarity, cause-and-effect, and systematization; children aged 3-5 years learn to understand sign systems (letters, numbers, and the connection between letters and speech). Their interest in learning deepens.

They learn to interact with each other and to follow and establish rules. Team role-playing and problem-solving become fundamentally important. Similar to toddlers, it is important for preschoolers to learn independently - through movement, sense of touch, and creating something with their own hands.

WHY STEAM?

  1. Since the beginning of the 21st century, the world has needed scientists capable of working in the field of innovative technologies.
  2. It has become clear that traditional subject-based educational programs do not sufficiently cover this need - subjects and disciplines are not perceived holistically and become fragmented information for the child.
  3. The STEAM approach creates strong logical links between subjects, promotes a global view of the world, and teaches children to notice patterns and correlations.
  4. Successful professionals of the future must combine and continuously develop the skills of an inventor, scientist, manager, and psychologist, all of which are touched by STEAM's innovative approach.
  5. STEAM education has a favorable effect on children's self-esteem and values: they see how different spheres of life intertwine, and this gives them a feeling of security, logic, and societal awareness.

LEGO® Engineering program

ages 4-5

This program includes a diverse curriculum that involves STEAM learning principles. It has both beginner and intermediate level lessons. One of the main goals of this program is to teach kids how to think like scientists as they build LEGO® models. In this class, we challenge kids with strategic questions to guide them through the process of applying science, technology, engineering, art, and math skills and test their ideas to answer different questions.

Furthermore, the building activities will reinforce the children’s creativity as we use LEGO® models in storytelling and acting.

LITTLE SCIENTIST

ages 4-6

Little Scientist is a STEAM-based program for kids who love science and are curious about the world around them. The goal of this program is to teach students through project-based learning how to combine each of the five disciplines (Science, Technology, Engineering, Art, and Math) and foster an inclusive learning environment in which all kids are able to engage and contribute.
